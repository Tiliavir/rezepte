# Spaghetti Carbonara (Classic Roman Style, US Units)

A rich, silky pasta dish made without cream: just eggs, cheese, pork fat, pepper, and hot pasta water.  
Done right, Carbonara tastes deeply savory, peppery, and luxurious while still feeling simple and rustic.

---

## Yield
- Serves: 4
- Time: 25–30 minutes

---

## Ingredients

### Core Ingredients
- **12 oz spaghetti**
    - Choose a high-quality bronze-cut pasta if possible; the rough surface helps the sauce cling.
    - Examples: De Cecco, Rummo, Garofalo.
- **6 oz guanciale, diced into small strips**
    - Traditional choice: cured pork jowl with rich, sweet fat and deep flavor.
    - If unavailable, use pancetta; thick-cut bacon is a distant backup (less traditional, smokier).
- **2 large eggs + 2 large egg yolks**
    - The yolks give the sauce body and gloss; whole eggs keep it balanced.
    - Very fresh eggs are ideal.
- **1 cup (about 3.5 oz) finely grated Pecorino Romano**
    - Salty, sheep’s milk cheese with sharp character; this is the classic Carbonara flavor.
- **1/4 cup (about 1 oz) finely grated Parmigiano-Reggiano** (optional but recommended)
    - Rounds out Pecorino with nuttier sweetness.
- **1 1/2 tsp freshly cracked black pepper**, plus more to taste
    - Toasting and cracking fresh pepper makes a major difference.
- **Kosher salt**, for pasta water (light hand—cheese and pork are already salty)

### Optional Finishing Touches (Use Sparingly)
- Extra Pecorino for serving
- A final pinch of coarse black pepper
- 1–2 tsp reserved guanciale fat drizzled on top for extra richness

---

## Why These Ingredients Matter (Quick Examples)

- **Guanciale vs Pancetta vs Bacon**
    - Guanciale: most traditional, richest mouthfeel, clean pork flavor.
    - Pancetta: milder, still excellent.
    - Bacon: smoky and salty; can dominate, so use less pepper and less salt.
- **Pecorino Romano**
    - Gives Carbonara its signature sharp, salty edge.
    - If too intense for your taste, use 3/4 cup Pecorino + 1/2 cup Parmesan.
- **Egg Ratio**
    - More yolks = creamier, more luxurious sauce.
    - More whole eggs = lighter texture and less intense richness.

---

## Instructions

1. **Boil the pasta**
     - Bring a large pot of water to a boil.
     - Salt lightly (about 1 tbsp kosher salt for 4 quarts water; less than usual).
     - Cook spaghetti until just shy of al dente, about 1 minute under package instructions.
     - Reserve **1 1/2 cups pasta water** before draining.

2. **Render the guanciale**
     - While pasta cooks, place guanciale in a large skillet over medium heat.
     - Cook 6–8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until fat renders and meat is crisp at edges.
     - Turn off heat and let skillet cool slightly (important so eggs don’t scramble later).

3. **Make the egg-cheese mixture**
     - In a bowl, whisk eggs + yolks, Pecorino, Parmesan (if using), and black pepper.
     - Add 2–3 tbsp warm pasta water and whisk until smooth and thick.
     - Texture should resemble a pourable paste.

4. **Combine pasta and guanciale**
     - Drain pasta and add immediately to the skillet with guanciale.
     - Toss well so noodles are coated in rendered fat.

5. **Create the sauce (off heat)**
     - Wait 20–30 seconds so pan heat drops slightly.
     - Add egg-cheese mixture and toss vigorously.
     - Add reserved pasta water a splash at a time until sauce turns glossy and coats each strand.
     - The sauce should be fluid, not clumpy, and not soupy.

6. **Adjust and serve immediately**
     - Taste before adding salt.
     - Add more pepper or cheese as desired.
     - Serve hot in warm bowls.

---

## Texture Guide (How to Tell It’s Right)

- **Too thick / clumpy:** add more hot pasta water and toss.
- **Too thin:** toss over very low heat for 10–20 seconds (careful not to scramble eggs).
- **Scrambled egg bits forming:** pan is too hot; immediately add a splash of water and toss off heat.

---

## Common Variations (Still Good, Less Traditional)

- **Extra creamy style:** add 1 extra yolk.
- **Milder cheese profile:** increase Parmesan and reduce Pecorino.
- **Spicy edge:** tiny pinch of red pepper flakes with black pepper.
- **Different pasta shapes:** rigatoni or tonnarelli hold sauce beautifully.

---

## Serving Ideas

- Simple green salad with lemon vinaigrette
- Roasted asparagus or broccolini
- Crusty bread for wiping the bowl
- Sparkling water with lemon, or a crisp dry white wine

---

## Storage Notes

Carbonara is best eaten fresh.  
If needed, refrigerate leftovers up to 1 day and reheat gently with a splash of water over very low heat.  
Do not microwave on high—eggs can overcook and become grainy.
